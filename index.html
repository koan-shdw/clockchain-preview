What is Clockchain

A verifiable global time standard: time you can prove.

A decentralized network of trusted time sources, from certified atomic clocks to national time institutes and satellite navigation systems, aggregated by AI and validated on-chain every second. Every timestamp is cryptographically signed, permanent, and independently verifiable by anyone.

Use Cases

Where a verifiable moment matters.

When precise ordering, auditability, and reconciliation are a requirement, a timestamp anyone can independently prove changes what is possible.

01

Compliance & auditability

Immutable, verifiable timestamps support regulatory investigations and dispute resolution in the financial sector by creating independent proof of when orders, trades, and market events occurred. Clockchain timestamps are also built to help meet strict new AI regulations.

Finance · Regulation · Audit
02

Chain of custody & proof of provenance

Trusted timestamps provide traceability across the supply chain for shipment handoffs, inspections, and environmental readings. In the legal sphere they prove when digital evidence existed and whether it has been altered. Content creators can prove when photos, video, audio, or documents were created, strengthening provenance and guarding against deepfakes and AI-generated media.

Supply chain · Legal · Media
03

Interoperability & a unified temporal system

Clockchain enables temporal interoperability: the ability for diverse systems, organizations, and AI agents to share one common, cryptographically verifiable understanding of time. The result is a unified temporal system that works across organizations, applications, cloud platforms, and distributed systems.

Interop · AI agents · Infrastructure
